About Me

After completing my Masters, I hope to pursue a PhD so that I can gain a deeper understanding of the theory behind the practice and apply my skills to solve more complex problems. Current Projects I am Working On

Robust, Individualized, and Structured Document Generation

My current project involves building a hierarchical document model that can effectively generate structured documents. This is in contrast to free-text generation models, such as the ones used by many Large Language Models, which often lack factual accuracy. My goal is to build a model that can aggregate large volumes of structured and unstructured information, such as webpages, papers, and user-created documents, and generate accurate and informative documents. The goal is to be able to individualize the document generation process so that each document is tailored to the specific needs of the user and is influenced primarily by the user's preferences as well as the content that is being aggregated, which should also be selected by the user. Essentially, the idea is to be able to individualize the models so that they are fine-tuned to each user, thereby providing more robust and accurate document generation outputs. The specific part of this project that I have been most interested in lately is the modeling of factual information and methods for better measuring the level of factuality of Transformer model generated output.
